About me

I build things to understand them.

I got into web development before I even knew it was a career path. I was tinkering with WordPress sites in school, and I just kept getting lost in it — tweaking layouts, connecting domains, figuring out why something looked one way on my screen and different on someone else's.

What I genuinely enjoy is understanding how things work: how a domain maps to a server, how DNS propagates, how a page actually ends up in a browser. I like experimenting with layouts and UI, especially when it involves making something feel clean and intuitive.

Right now I'm studying Life Sciences and Data Science, and I find that combination interesting — it keeps me thinking across different fields. Building things with visible output keeps me motivated more than anything else.

My motivation

Why I applied to IMG

While I was going through IMG's website, I found myself thinking about the structure — how information was organised, what the navigation felt like, whether a newcomer could easily find what they needed. I started thinking about what it would take to make it cleaner, more modern, and easier to navigate, while still keeping the identity that makes it feel like IMG.

That's what made me want to apply. Not because I think anything is broken, but because I saw it as a space where good design decisions could genuinely matter — and where I'd learn a lot while contributing to something real.

— Lokesh Prajapat, Website Team Application
